## Unique Value Proposition

**Primary UVP:** "We build the content engine that lets marketing teams ship high-performing video at scale, without freelancer chaos or in-house overhead."

**The real product:** Marketing leaders are not buying video. They are buying career insurance. When content slips, leadership blames the manager who chose the vendor. Every messaging decision should reflect that.

## Five Differentiators (use these in copy)

1. **Volume AND quality, no tradeoff** — Subscription editors do volume, agencies do quality, Viral does both at scale.
2. **Strategic creative guidance, not order-taking** — Platform-specific expertise, hook strategy, performance thinking before the edit.
3. **Built for scale from day one** — Structured pods, managing editors, QA checkpoints, standardized workflows.
4. **System over talent** — No dependency on unicorn editors. The system survives any one editor leaving.
5. **Speed without sacrificing quality** — Same-day first drafts, predictable turnaround, Slack response within one hour on business days.

## Messaging & Voice Guardrails (2026 Brand Book)

Every cold email must pass these checks before it ships:

- **Calm and confident.** Never urgent or hype-driven.
- **Direct.** Lead with the point. No fluff, no hedging, no corporate speak.
- **Specific.** "We respond within one hour on business days" beats "we're responsive."
- **You over we.** The client's world is the center of the conversation.
- **Active voice always.** "Our team delivers same-day" not "first drafts are delivered same-day."
- **Grade 8–10 reading level.** Short words, clear sentences.

**On-brand phrases (use freely):** "Here's the plan." | "We know what works." | "What actually moves the needle." | "We build content engines, not single videos." | "Plug in and trust it." | "Clarity, not chaos."

**Banned language:** revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive, world-class, best-in-class, cutting-edge, crush, dominate, 10x, skyrocket, hack the algorithm, blow up your brand, synergy, holistic, leverage (verb), guru, ninja, rockstar, white-glove, bespoke, elite, authentic storytelling, elevate your brand, drive awareness, telling your story.

## Signals & Triggers (list-first sourcing)

**Hiring signals (strongest single category):**
- New CMO / VP Marketing / Head of Content posted on LinkedIn — incoming leaders reset content stack inside 90 days
- Open role: Video Editor, Content Producer, Social Media Manager — admits production is the bottleneck
- Open role: Paid Social Manager / Performance Marketer — paid social runs on creative volume
- Open role: Freelance / Contract Video Editor — active confession of a content gap

**Funding and growth:** Series A–C round, revenue/headcount growth, acquisition or merger, geographic expansion.

**Product and content:** New product launch, new podcast/webinar/YouTube channel, new website launch, increased Meta/LinkedIn ad spend.

**Vertical-specific:** FDA approvals (pharma), new product line (DTC), new agency client win (agencies), new partner announcement (pro services).

**Do NOT contact:** DIY creators, budget shoppers benchmarking against Fiverr, wedding videographers, music video producers, hype-driven startup-bro founders, political campaigns, gambling, micromanagers, full-service expectation buyers, organizationally chaotic teams, solo attorneys.

## What's Working (so far)

<!-- Sync: 2026-05-12 -->

Based on the 32+ interested replies from the pre-Kinetyca outbound (Jan–May 2026):

- **Local Philadelphia framing** is generating replies from Philly-based agencies and businesses. Several positive replies came from Philly addresses (e.g., Devine Partners, Cohere Studio, Actual Size in Pittsburgh PA).
- **"Quick question" agency-focused hook** (Michael Kaneff variant) is producing replies from agency CEOs/heads of production who occasionally bring in outside crews — exactly the Daniel persona. Examples: Red House Communications, BLINK Advertising, Tango Multimedia, Devine Partners, Actual Size.
- **McKesson + Asset Map as paired social proof** lands enough to get example requests. This is the most-used proof combo in current outbound.
- **One-line follow-ups ("just wanted to circle back")** with a slight value-add convert silent prospects to "send samples" replies.
- **Healthcare names** (McKesson, Janssen, Cortechs.ai) per the playbook are the strongest asset Viral has — and they are currently underutilized in copy (only "McKesson and Asset Map" appears).

## What's Not Working (so far)

<!-- Sync: 2026-05-12 -->

Patterns visible in the pre-Kinetyca outbound that need correction before Kinetyca's launch:

- **Target mismatch with GTM playbook.** Current outbound is hitting real estate brokers, a harm reduction nonprofit, a dental office, restaurants, wedding photographers, and PR agencies — most fall on the playbook's "do not contact" list. ICP discipline is missing.
- **Repeated social proof drift.** Same "McKesson and Asset Map" combo in every email. Underutilizes the J&J, Janssen, Self Financial, eBay, Cortechs.ai, Inbody, Sotheby's, Chevrolet, Procter & Gamble logos. Every email feels identical.
- **Apologetic PS line.** "PS. If you don't care, just let me know and I won't reach out again" violates the brand book's "calm and confident" rule. It reads insecure, not professional.
- **"Small video production team" framing** contradicts the actual product — 83-person fully-remote team that has edited 100,000+ videos. Underselling the differentiators (system, scale, QA, managing editors).
- **No vertical-specific case studies.** A PR firm in NYC gets the same McKesson/Asset Map line as a wellness coach in Philly. Playbook explicitly says "MSP targets need MSP case studies."
- **No 90-day signal-first targeting.** Hiring signals (new CMO, open video editor role) are explicitly the strongest single category per playbook — and not used at all in current outbound.
- **Domain rotation without sender consistency.** Each email goes from a different domain variant (tryviralidea.com, teamviralidea.com, sayviralidea.com, ideavirals.com, viralidea-collective.com, etc.). This is fine for warmup but reads as inconsistent to anyone Googling the sender.

Detailed BEFORE/AFTER analysis in [optimization/copy-evolution-pre-kinetyca.md](optimization/copy-evolution-pre-kinetyca.md).
